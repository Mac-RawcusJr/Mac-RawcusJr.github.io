Neuroplasicity and Growth mindset

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Studies have shown that those who understand that the brain is malleable and changing escape a fixed mindset that lets them believe they are born with intelligence and cannot be worked on. By allowing kids to understand that the brain can change thy are more motivated to learn and put in the effort. Neuro-mythological barriers is a term used to define the mental blocks that hold people back from growing. These barriers are put in place subconsciously by ourselves due to the belief of not being able to do something so we put up barriers to stop training in these areas and over time they begin to solidify getting stronger and more difficult to breakdown. Much like the saying "can't teach on old dog new tricks" adults have tougher times trying to understand new information and adapt their thinking, especially if they believe that they don't have the ability to do so. This is why teaching kids in early school years about the concept of neuroplasticity can build more highways than barriers making them believe that they are smart enough or strong enough.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit

The principles of neuroplasticity state that the brains functionality and shape can be grow, change and evolve through training and experiences. Most commonly the brain is trained sub-consciously so if we were to start training the brain to the way we want and full consciously then the brain would grow even faster. Training the brain is the exact same as training any other muscle you’ve got to start off small get the technique right to train the right areas and then slowly increase the frequency and strain of the exercise. The more you exercise the stronger you get. Although the brain can't physically lift weights and run for a kilometre we can use it and challenge it by using mental situations. Examples of mental challenges that can train the brain are things like, crossword puzzles, learning new skills travelling for new experiences and Video Gaming. Therefore to train my brain I engage with making new experiences to develop new neural pathways and learning new skills that help me to strengthen those neural pathways.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Practice makes Perfect is a great technique for improving a brains neuroplasticity because the more we practice the more familiar we are with an activity and the better we can visualise to improve. Which brings us to another method to improving neuroplasticity, Visualisation. Visualisation allows your brains neuro signals to travel down those pathways without having to actually perform the task physically. Although the improvement is typically only 22% with visualisation an activity it is still very impressive considering that little physical movements are made.

Growth Mindset
Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

A growth mindset is a mental state where a person can embrace change and new experiences and use them to improve and grow their own skills. As opposed to a fixed mindset where the person believes they are born with skills and no room for improvement denies change and new experiences. A growth mindset is very important because being able to accept advice, corrections and failure allows the person to learn from their mistake and improve their growing skill set.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

During this exploration, I have found that there is a strong understanding as to what a growth mindset is and how we can influence children to take on a stronger growth mindset and prepare them which is amazing and will give them great opportunities to learn from in their future. However, it is a lot harder to change an adults mindset and being adults we learn the importance of a growth mindset and the impact it has but is much harder to develop one. So for those students less fortunate to develop a growth mindset in their younger years struggle later on as adults due to the lack of growth mindset influence strengthening neuropathways that go against a growth mindset and therefore making it more difficult to change later on in life when they begin to realise the importance of a changing mindset.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

Integrating a growth mindset into a learning journey is not easy for those who do not have a sub-conscious growth mindset. Being a person who's default mindset resorts to negativity and a more fixed mindset it takes more effort and control to maintain a growth mindset when learning new skills. Therefore, to integrate a growth mindset into my learning to do my best to make the learning tasks enjoyable and make games out of them. Have clear small objectives and celebrate them as I go. While also acknowledging that I am going to fail somethings and not get everything correct but I can always try again, not forever but just till I get it right and understand that when I do I'll never get it wrong again.

Emotional Intelligence

My definition of Emtional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence is the capacity to acknowledge and not act on emotions while also handling interpersonal relationship empathically and respectfully without judgment.

Difference from IQ

Simply put the difference between IQ and EQ IQ is what we think, and EQ is how we think. IQ is the ability to solve problems, use logic, understand, and resolve complex ideas using one’s intelligence. Whereas EQ is the ability to recognize emotion absorb information without judgement and adapt your actions to emotion of yourself and others.

The best visual example of this I find is a clip from the TV show young Sheldon when Sheldon and his twin sister (Missy) are begin tested and given the same test in different rooms.

As we can see in the video clip Sheldon excels with logical and mathematical problem solving and Missy excels at identifying the emotions of the apes shown in the picture and throughout the clip display emotional intelligence with the professor.

Why is EQ important?

Benefits of emotional intelligence are that you’re it heightens ones understanding of themselves and others. This is a benefit because by understanding what emotions you are feeling and how to adapt to each one stops them from taking over and becoming confused and unfocused. For example, If you got angry you would be able to prevent yourself from lashing out, punching things, or even hurting people. Knowing when you are angry what can happen and how to avoid negative situations can be very advantageous when working on projects and problem solving.

But there is also the ability to understand the emotions of others, recognising when someone is sad or angry, we can use emotional intelligence to help them adapt to their own emotions. Which can improve productivity within a workplace.

A great example of the benefits of interpersonal skills is Elon Musk’s who openly states that “he would rather a group of slightly intelligent people who work well together rather than a group of highly intelligent people who do not work well together”. This is because the group who work well together can understand respect and acknowledge each other’s strengths and weaknesses leading to productivity. Whereas the group of highly intelligent people often have tendencies to prove their the smartest and will avoid work with others and not asking for help, slowing down productivity.

Personal Experience of handling tension wihtin a group

During my time at Cromwell College, I participated in a programme called B.L.A.S.T. which was essentially a way to give high school students a military experience. Doing drills, marching practice, and circuit physical training. towards the end of this programme we were all spilt into small groups of 6-7 and were to run a orientation course across the time for most of the day. I, being the eldest in the programme was given the task of Team Leader and given the more troublesome students. The ones that would not get along with anyone and rebel as often as they could. Some may think this was unfair, but the head teacher and I had already discussed this plan as to not allow these students to disrupt the other groups.

Now during the orientation activity these students consistently argued and fought and were being very disruptive, not allowing us to complete tasks or move efficiently. Unfortunately, i did have not use a more aggressive leadership style and tell the students what to do instead of suggesting and assign tasks appropriately. Once I had separated the two more troublesome students and given them simplistic and meaningful tasks such as setting the speed of the group and giving the best direction.

The techniques I used to handle the tension and conflict within the group was to give everyone their own tasks away from each other to avoid collisions. Unfortunately I did have to take away the students right to speak up because he had lost the trust and reliance of his team mates.

These methods to stop the disruptive behaviour of the students was surprisingly effective and to not only our teams surprise by the surprise of all those watching our team managed to win the orientation and complete the course much more efficiently than the other teams. Reflecting on how I acted in that situations I’m proud that I was able to take charge more firmly and get the students to start working productively. However, I did not enjoy having to be angry with the students and taking away their opportunities to speak up.
