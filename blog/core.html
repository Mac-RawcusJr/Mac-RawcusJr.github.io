Emotional Intelligence

My definition of Emtional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence is the capacity to acknowledge and not act on emotions while also handling interpersonal relationship empathically and respectfully without judgment.

Difference from IQ

Simply put the difference between IQ and EQ IQ is what we think, and EQ is how we think. IQ is the ability to solve problems, use logic, understand, and resolve complex ideas using one’s intelligence. Whereas EQ is the ability to recognize emotion absorb information without judgement and adapt your actions to emotion of yourself and others.

The best visual example of this I find is a clip from the TV show young Sheldon when Sheldon and his twin sister (Missy) are begin tested and given the same test in different rooms.

As we can see in the video clip Sheldon excels with logical and mathematical problem solving and Missy excels at identifying the emotions of the apes shown in the picture and throughout the clip display emotional intelligence with the professor.

Why is EQ important?

Benefits of emotional intelligence are that you’re it heightens ones understanding of themselves and others. This is a benefit because by understanding what emotions you are feeling and how to adapt to each one stops them from taking over and becoming confused and unfocused. For example, If you got angry you would be able to prevent yourself from lashing out, punching things, or even hurting people. Knowing when you are angry what can happen and how to avoid negative situations can be very advantageous when working on projects and problem solving.

But there is also the ability to understand the emotions of others, recognising when someone is sad or angry, we can use emotional intelligence to help them adapt to their own emotions. Which can improve productivity within a workplace.

A great example of the benefits of interpersonal skills is Elon Musk’s who openly states that “he would rather a group of slightly intelligent people who work well together rather than a group of highly intelligent people who do not work well together”. This is because the group who work well together can understand respect and acknowledge each other’s strengths and weaknesses leading to productivity. Whereas the group of highly intelligent people often have tendencies to prove their the smartest and will avoid work with others and not asking for help, slowing down productivity.

Ethical Decision

A strong ethical decision we all must make is where we want to go in life and what we want to do. These decisions have many different influences from various sources such as family friends, government, laws, and of course personal
For example. For a long time during high school, I always wanted to work in the adventure tourism industry because I would be required o do physical work and have fun jumping of cliffs, riding down mountains and being in nature. This fed my Balance, Adventure, Experience and Connection values.
Because of these values I then choose to attend a Tertiary study programme that would better equip myself to join and thrive in the industry. This led me to researching and experiencing what different tertiary programmes had to offer and I needed to decide which one best suited me and lined up with my values.
My 3 choices became Otago Uni, Queenstown Resort College (QRC) and New Zealand School of Tourism (NZST). Each provided me with strong Experience and knowledge of the industry which aligned with two of my values.

Otago – focus on the skills of the outdoor adventure guide for kayaking, rock climbing, tramping, skiing – all great areas that would allow me to travel, meet and learn with likeminded people (Adventure and Connection)

NZST – focus on extreme outdoor sports and extreme conditions, smaller course intake (less connection, narrow experience, independence)

QRC – focus on Adventure Tourism more about how to run a tourism business - marketing, HR, business financials plus experience in a huge variety of outdoor pursuits (adventure, independence, connection). It offered a more whole person and whole business opportunity.

After weighing my personal values and the values I would get out of each provider, I decided to go with QRC as it aligned with more of my values allowing for the best fit.

Personal Experience of handling tension wihtin a group

During my time at Cromwell College, I participated in a programme called B.L.A.S.T. which was essentially a way to give high school students a military experience. Doing drills, marching practice, and circuit physical training. towards the end of this programme we were all spilt into small groups of 6-7 and were to run a orientation course across the time for most of the day. I, being the eldest in the programme was given the task of Team Leader and given the more troublesome students. The ones that would not get along with anyone and rebel as often as they could. Some may think this was unfair, but the head teacher and I had already discussed this plan as to not allow these students to disrupt the other groups.

Now during the orientation activity these students consistently argued and fought and were being very disruptive, not allowing us to complete tasks or move efficiently. Unfortunately, i did have not use a more aggressive leadership style and tell the students what to do instead of suggesting and assign tasks appropriately. Once I had separated the two more troublesome students and given them simplistic and meaningful tasks such as setting the speed of the group and giving the best direction.

The techniques I used to handle the tension and conflict within the group was to give everyone their own tasks away from each other to avoid collisions. Unfortunately I did have to take away the students right to speak up because he had lost the trust and reliance of his team mates.

These methods to stop the disruptive behaviour of the students was surprisingly effective and to not only our teams surprise by the surprise of all those watching our team managed to win the orientation and complete the course much more efficiently than the other teams. Reflecting on how I acted in that situations I’m proud that I was able to take charge more firmly and get the students to start working productively. However, I did not enjoy having to be angry with the students and taking away their opportunities to speak up.
